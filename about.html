About me

My name is Nicholas Krämer. I am an MSc student of mathematics at the University of Bonn and a part-time research assistant at the Institute for Numerical Simulation. I am currently writing up my thesis in which I investigate questions related to Gaussian process emulators in Bayesian inverse problems (for example see this link).

Research Interests

My research interests probably lie somewhere between numerical analysis and computational statistics. One of the central challenges I am intrigued by is to quantify impacts of different levels of uncertainty in complex models.

  • Uncertainty quantification
  • Probabilistic numerics
  • Gaussian processes
  • Bayesian statistics and Bayesian inverse problems
